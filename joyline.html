BORN A CRIME

Trevor Noah

Stories from a South African childhood

"No Place To Belong"

The above title could be a perfect alternative title for Born a crime book by Trevor Noah. This is because most of the time Trevor Noah felt like an outsider. Trevor’s feeling was greatly influenced by his mother’s choice to break the European Immortality act to have a child with a Swiss man called Robert. Trevor was “too white” to fit in the black kids and too “too black” to fit in the white kids.Trevor fits in both categories as he could speak fluent English, Zulu, Xhosa and Africaans.

"Abel wanted a traditional marriage with a traditional wife… but he never fell in love with subservient women… 'He only wants a woman who is free because his dream is to put her in a cage.'”

Abel Ngisaveni was Patricia’s second husband who started living with them after Trevor and his friends burned down the house of a white man while playing with a magnifying glass and matches. After Abel and Patricia got married, he became insecure of their relationship with Robert and Trevor could now not see his father any more.

While gender emphasizes on gender equality and traditional expectations from the women,Patricia goes contrary to all of these set bars. She leaves home and trains to become a secretary for her oppressors. Patricia’s fierce resilient nature in life is strange to Abel. Abel expects a woman who is care free, extremely submissive and who views her businesses as less important. In the real world, most women are encouraged to be ambitious, independent and assertive but in reality, society views this as too much or that they're violating some societal norms. Women are expected to strictly adhere to their gender roles while men are not. Moving forward as the society continues to evolve, we hope that more men and women will be empowered so as not to reflect Abel’s Mindset.
